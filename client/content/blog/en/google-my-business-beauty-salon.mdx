<h2 id="why-google-business-matters">Why Google Business Profile Is Non-Negotiable</h2>

<p>When someone searches "lash extensions near me" or "beauty salon [your city]," Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) determines whether your salon appears in the local results — the map pack that shows above regular search results. For most beauty businesses, this listing drives more new client inquiries than any other single channel, including Instagram and paid advertising.</p>

<p>Yet most beauty professionals either haven't claimed their listing, haven't optimized it, or set it up once and never touched it again. This represents a massive missed opportunity. An optimized Google Business Profile is free, works around the clock, and directly connects you with people actively searching for your exact services in your area.</p>

<h2 id="claiming-your-profile">Setting Up and Claiming Your Profile</h2>

<h3 id="initial-setup">Initial Setup</h3>

<p>If you haven't already, go to business.google.com and either claim your existing listing or create a new one. Google will verify your business through a postcard, phone call, or email verification. This typically takes 5–14 days. Don't skip this step — unverified profiles have limited visibility and functionality.</p>

<h3 id="choosing-categories">Choosing the Right Categories</h3>

<p>Your primary category is the most important ranking factor you control. Choose the most specific category that matches your primary service. "Eyelash Salon" is better than "Beauty Salon" if lash work is your focus. You can add up to 9 additional categories — add all that apply: "Beauty Salon," "Nail Salon," "Waxing Service," etc.</p>

<p>Don't add categories for services you don't offer. Google penalizes irrelevant categories, and clients who arrive expecting services you don't provide leave negative reviews.</p>

<h2 id="optimizing-your-listing">Optimizing Every Section</h2>

<h3 id="business-name">Business Name</h3>

<p>Use your actual registered business name — nothing more. Don't stuff keywords into your business name ("Jane's Lash Studio - Best Lash Extensions in Miami"). Google actively penalizes keyword-stuffed names and may suspend your listing.</p>

<h3 id="business-description">Business Description</h3>

<p>You have 750 characters. Use them strategically. Lead with what you do and who you serve. Include your primary services, specialties, and location naturally — not as a keyword list. Example:</p>

<p>"Specializing in classic, volume, and hybrid lash extensions in downtown Tbilisi. Our certified lash artists create customized looks for every eye shape, from natural enhancement to full glam. Walk-ins welcome. Online booking available."</p>

<h3 id="services-and-products">Services and Products</h3>

<p>Add every service you offer with descriptions and prices. Google uses this information to match your listing with specific searches. A client searching "volume lash extensions" is more likely to find you if you've listed "Volume Lash Extensions" as a service with a description. Include pricing — profiles with prices get more engagement than those without.</p>

<h3 id="hours-and-contact">Hours and Contact Information</h3>

<p>Keep your hours accurate and updated. Mark holiday hours, vacation closures, and special hours in advance. Incorrect hours lead to frustrated clients showing up to a closed business — and negative reviews. Add your phone number, website, and booking link. The appointment URL field lets you link directly to your online booking system.</p>

<h2 id="photos-strategy">Photo Strategy</h2>

<p>Listings with photos receive 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks than those without, according to Google's own data. Quality and quantity both matter.</p>

<h3 id="essential-photos">Essential Photos to Upload</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Exterior:</strong> 2–3 photos of your storefront from different angles, including your signage. This helps clients find you physically.</li>
<li><strong>Interior:</strong> 3–5 photos showing your workspace, waiting area, and overall ambiance. Clean, well-lit, and inviting.</li>
<li><strong>Team:</strong> Professional headshots of yourself and any team members. People connect with faces.</li>
<li><strong>Work samples:</strong> 10–20 of your best before and after photos. These are your primary conversion tool — upload your strongest portfolio work.</li>
<li><strong>Logo and cover:</strong> Your business logo and a high-quality cover photo that represents your brand.</li>
</ul>

<h3 id="photo-quality">Photo Quality Standards</h3>

<p>All photos should be well-lit, sharp, and accurately represent your business. Use Glow.GE to enhance your portfolio photos before uploading — consistent, professional-quality images across your listing significantly improve the impression your profile makes. Avoid dark, blurry, or heavily filtered photos. Google can reject low-quality images, and clients will scroll past them.</p>

<p>Add new photos regularly — at least 2–4 per month. Google favors active listings, and fresh content keeps your profile looking current.</p>

<h2 id="reviews-strategy">Review Strategy</h2>

<h3 id="why-reviews-matter">Why Reviews Matter</h3>

<p>Reviews are the second most important ranking factor for local search. Businesses with more reviews and higher ratings rank higher and convert more viewers into clients. A salon with 47 reviews averaging 4.8 stars will almost always outperform a salon with 6 reviews averaging 5.0 stars.</p>

<h3 id="getting-more-reviews">Getting More Reviews</h3>

<p>The most effective strategy is simple: ask every satisfied client to leave a review, and make it easy. Create a direct review link (found in your Google Business dashboard under "Ask for reviews") and share it via:</p>

<ul>
<li>A follow-up text message sent 2–4 hours after their appointment</li>
<li>A QR code displayed at your station or checkout area</li>
<li>A card handed to clients as they leave with the QR code and a simple "We'd love your feedback"</li>
</ul>

<p>Timing matters. Clients are most enthusiastic about their results in the first few hours after their appointment. A message like "Hi [name], thank you for visiting today! If you love your new lashes, we'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps other clients find us" is simple, genuine, and effective.</p>

<h3 id="responding-to-reviews">Responding to Reviews</h3>

<p>Respond to every review — positive and negative. For positive reviews, a brief, personal thank-you is sufficient. For negative reviews, respond promptly, professionally, and empathetically. Acknowledge the issue, apologize for the experience, and offer to resolve it offline. Never argue, blame the client, or get defensive in a public response. Potential clients read your responses as much as the reviews themselves.</p>

<h2 id="google-posts">Using Google Posts</h2>

<p>Google Posts are short updates that appear directly on your listing. Think of them as social media posts, but on Google. Types of posts you can create:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>What's new:</strong> Share updates, new services, or behind-the-scenes content</li>
<li><strong>Offers:</strong> Promote discounts or special packages with an expiration date</li>
<li><strong>Events:</strong> Announce workshops, open house events, or seasonal promotions</li>
</ul>

<p>Post at least once per week. Each post stays visible for 7 days (6 months for events). Include a photo, a clear description, and a call-to-action button ("Book now," "Learn more," "Call now"). Regular posting signals to Google that your business is active, which can improve your ranking.</p>

<h2 id="qa-section">Managing the Q&A Section</h2>

<p>Your Google listing has a Q&A section where anyone can ask and answer questions. Proactively seed it with common questions and provide your own answers: "Do you accept walk-ins?" "What's your cancellation policy?" "How long do lash extensions last?" This gives potential clients instant answers and prevents incorrect information from being posted by random users.</p>

<p>Monitor this section regularly. If someone asks a question, answer it within 24 hours. Unanswered questions look neglected.</p>

<h2 id="tracking-performance">Tracking Performance</h2>

<p>Google Business Profile provides built-in analytics showing how many people viewed your listing, what searches they used to find you, how many clicked for directions, called, or visited your website. Review these monthly. Key metrics to watch:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Search queries:</strong> What terms are people using to find you? This informs your service descriptions and website SEO.</li>
<li><strong>Actions:</strong> Are people calling, requesting directions, or visiting your website? Low action rates relative to views suggest your listing needs better photos, reviews, or descriptions.</li>
<li><strong>Photo views:</strong> Compare your photo views to competitors in your category. If you're below average, add more and better photos.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="common-mistakes">Common Mistakes to Avoid</h2>

<ul>
<li>Keyword-stuffing your business name (risk of suspension)</li>
<li>Using a P.O. box or virtual address (Google requires a physical location where you serve customers)</li>
<li>Neglecting to update holiday hours (leads to negative reviews)</li>
<li>Ignoring negative reviews (looks unprofessional to potential clients)</li>
<li>Uploading low-quality or irrelevant photos</li>
<li>Setting up the profile once and never updating it</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="start-optimizing">Take Action This Week</h2>

<p>If you haven't claimed your profile, do it today. If you have, spend 30 minutes this week updating your description, adding fresh photos, and creating a review request message you can send to clients. These small actions compound over time and can become your most reliable source of new client inquiries — without spending a single dollar on advertising.</p>
